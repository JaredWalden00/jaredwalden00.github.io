It was about 3 years ago when I was itching to make something. I had just started working at my first internship as an IT-MSP provider for a small 3 and a half person company. As thankful as I was to work for them, my dreams of being a Software Developer still flourished above most of my other aspirations. I had heard about the colorful offices, cool software, and flourishing money that comes with Computer Science. However, through months of struggling and youtube videos, I managed to finally get my first Software Developer job at a transportation company. However I had the same situation, I still felt as if I was missing something.

As my software development skills got better, I decided to give Unity a shot. I already had experience with C# but had never done any sort of 3D development. However, I was a big fan of games like Destiny and Risk of Rain 2 so I wanted to give one a shot. My first project was creating a ball. Yep, a ball that just rolls. This was the first thing everyone on Reddit recommended so I just ran with it. I got bored of it very quickly though and dreamed much bigger. I remember telling someone at my second co-op how I really wanted to make a giant, ambitious 3D roguelike, and their response was to start small. As good as advice that was, I already had my mind set on going big.

I watched a lot of different character locomotion tutorials and discovered more and more about the capabilities Unity held. My goal though was to use as little premade assets as possible, I wanted to learn from the ground up. Sebastian Graves became my go to man as his Elden Rings in Unity tutorials became an everyday routine for me. I didn’t want to make a Souls game, but I knew a lot of the mechanics in it were stuff that I would very likely use and modify.

Between my second and third co-op, my motivation fell. I had dreamed too big yet couldn’t even imagine in my head what a XYZ plane even looked like. Silly, I know, yet I felt as if I was missing something. Not long after that, I dived into drawing. I followed Draw a Box’s course which incorporated mainly 3D spaces into designs. I thought that working through this would not only make me a better artist but also a better visualizer. Eventually then by the end of my third co-op I felt well equipped and hopped back into Unity.

Since that day, I’ve dedicated hours and hours to creating my vision for a game. I’ve created my own system of taking on tasks each day and weighing whether or not the time was worth it for certain features. I learned Blender in that time also which is still definitely a struggle, however it’s showing to be extremely useful. A lot of days, I feel overwhelmed by the vision for what I want, but in the end I know I will never regret the experience and amount of knowledge I’ve gained through it.

I have a goal that before I graduate from college I want there to be a fully-fleshed out game. I want there to be a beginning and I want there to be an end. So far I’ve inched closer and closer everyday to gaining the end I want, but I still have ways to go. I want to use this blog to not only talk about what i’ve been doing but also what needs to get done. Anyways thank you for reading!
